Market Challenges

The unique pressures facing IDD providers

IDD service providers navigate complex challenges—from direct support professional shortages to evolving Medicaid regulations. Technology can help, but only when it's designed with this population's specific needs in mind.

Direct Support Professional Shortage

The DSP workforce crisis means providers struggle to maintain staffing levels. Technology must extend caregiver capacity—not add burden—to deliver quality support with fewer hands.

Geographic Barriers

Many IDD individuals live in rural areas with limited access to specialists. Travel time for staff reduces time spent on direct care. Remote solutions can bridge these gaps.

Complex Care Coordination

Coordinating medical appointments, behavioral support, medication management, and daily activities across multiple providers requires robust communication infrastructure.

Independence vs. Safety Balance

The goal is maximum independence while ensuring safety. Technology should empower individuals to live more independently without constant physical supervision.

Medicaid Reimbursement Changes

Waiver programs and HCBS settings rules continue to evolve. Providers need flexible technology that can adapt to changing documentation and service delivery requirements.

Scaling Quality Care

Growing to serve more individuals while maintaining personalized, high-quality care is the central tension. Technology must enable scale without sacrificing the human touch.
