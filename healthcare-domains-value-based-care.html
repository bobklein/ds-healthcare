Understanding VBC Technology

Technology that aligns incentives with outcomes

Value-based care models reward healthcare organizations for keeping patients healthy, not just treating them when they're sick. This fundamental shift requires new capabilities: risk stratification, care gap identification, quality measure tracking, and shared savings calculations.

Most EHRs weren't designed for value-based care. They capture clinical data but don't surface the insights needed to manage populations, identify at-risk patients, or optimize quality scores. Custom technology bridges this gap.

We've built VBC platforms that operate at scale-26,000+ patients across 7 states. Our solutions integrate with existing EHRs, aggregate data from multiple sources, and deliver the actionable intelligence that value-based success requires.

The Challenge

Why value-based care is hard without the right technology

Data Fragmentation

Patient data lives in multiple systems-EHRs, claims, labs, pharmacies. Without integration, you can't see the full picture of patient health and utilization.

Risk Adjustment Complexity

Accurate risk adjustment requires capturing all relevant diagnoses, ensuring proper documentation, and understanding HCC coding hierarchies.

Quality Measure Tracking

Dozens of quality measures with different numerators, denominators, and exclusions. Manual tracking is error-prone and resource-intensive.

Care Gap Identification

Proactive care requires knowing which patients need what interventions. Without real-time visibility, gaps slip through.

Attribution Challenges

Understanding which patients you're responsible for, reconciling attribution lists, and managing patient alignment.

Financial Modeling

Predicting shared savings, modeling different scenarios, understanding where you stand against benchmarks throughout the year.
