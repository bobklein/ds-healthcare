The Problem

Empty slots cost you twice—lost revenue and wasted capacity.

Healthcare no-show rates average 15-30% depending on specialty and patient population. Every empty slot represents lost revenue that can't be recovered—the physician is paid, the room is ready, but there's no patient to treat.

Traditional approaches—reminder calls, overbooking across the board, deposit requirements—are blunt instruments. They treat all patients the same when the reality is that no-show risk varies dramatically based on patient history, appointment type, lead time, and dozens of other factors.

AI-powered prediction identifies which specific appointments are high-risk before the day arrives. This enables surgical intervention: extra outreach for high-risk slots, smart overbooking only where it's safe, and proactive rescheduling to fill gaps.

The math: A practice with 100 daily appointments and 20% no-show rate loses 20 slots per day. At $200 average revenue per visit, that's $4,000 daily or $1M+ annually. Reducing no-shows by 30% recovers $300K+ with no additional marketing or capacity.
